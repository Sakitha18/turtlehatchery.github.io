Habitat Loss

The destruction and alteration of turtle habitats, such as coastal areas, wetlands, and nesting beaches, pose a significant threat to turtles. Urban development, agriculture, deforestation, and pollution can all lead to the degradation and loss of their habitats...

Climate Change

Rising temperatures and changing weather patterns associated with climate change can have detrimental effects on turtles. For example, increasing temperatures can influence the sex ratio of hatchlings, as warmer temperatures tend to produce more females. Additionally, rising sea levels can flood nesting areas, reducing suitable nesting grounds for many species of turtles...

Overexploitation

Threat 03

Pollution

Pollution, including plastic debris, chemical pollutants, and oil spills, poses a serious threat to turtles. Turtles can mistake plastic bags and other litter for food, leading to ingestion and potential digestive blockages. Chemical pollutants and oil spills can contaminate their habitats, affecting their health and reproductive capabilities...
